Model Notes

  • Cap Rate affects asset valuation and NAV only — it does not change operating cash flows or distributions.
  • Occupancy scales NOI at the TitleCo building level before rolling up to the LP. Base case is 95%. A 10pp drop (95% → 85%) reduces NOI by ~10.5%.
  • Lease-Up Period reduces NOI in the stabilization year of each phase (Y4, Y6, Y8). A 24-month lease-up reduces stabilization-year NOI by ~50%.
  • Development Yield is NOI ÷ invested capital. TitleCo X as-modelled = 10.02%; TitleCo Y = 10.39%. Both require rent calibration to reach the LP target of 10.5%.
  • Debt Rate scales interest on the fixed debenture schedule (Series A–D, peak $987M). Y7 DSCR of 1.31× is the tightest base-case year; covenant floor is 1.20×.
  • Market Yield Rate is the distribution capitalization rate (proforma cell AC23) applied to Y8–Y10 distributions to derive Market Value. At base 8%, a $24.09/unit distribution implies a $301.15 market price. Lowering the yield (bull) raises Market Value; raising it (bear) compresses it. This slider also drives the Discount / Premium to NAV row.
  • Income Continuity (Y1–Y3) is an accounting provision of $3.05M–$3.5M/year that offsets the construction-period EBITDA deficit, keeping book NAV at par (~$100/unit). This is a committed general partner income support entry — confirm terms and whether amounts represent cash receipts or accrual entries.
  • Market Value Y1–Y3 is displayed at $100.00 (offering price / par). Units were issued under prospectus at this price; no secondary market formed during the construction period. Carrying pre-revenue LP units at offering price is the appropriate basis per standard LP accounting practice acceptable to institutional auditors.
  • Interest Coverage (DSCR) = EBITDA ÷ Net Interest (proforma row 74 formula). Y4 coverage of 2.02× is boosted by low early-phase debt; Y7 tightens to 1.31× as debenture drawdowns peak.
  • Compounded Return (Y8) = 14.775% is the base-case proforma value (row 27). This figure is not recomputed under stress — treat as indicative base case only.
  • Benetti Holdings dilution (10%) is held constant. Economic impact is embedded in the diluted unit count of 2,777,777.
  • Outstanding question: Advisory fee modelled flat at $2.5M/year. At 1% of gross funded value (~$1.2B by Y7), fee could reach $12M/year — confirm fee base with issuer.
