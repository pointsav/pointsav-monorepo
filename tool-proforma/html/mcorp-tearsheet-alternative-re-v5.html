Structure — Capital Stack and Priority of Claims

Capital Stack and Priority of Claims

The Limited Partnership issues First Secured Mortgage Debentures ("Debentures") that are registered as first-charge mortgages on title against portfolio properties. Debenture holders hold a senior secured position and are entitled to repayment of principal and accrued interest from asset proceeds in priority to all LP unitholders.

LP unitholders occupy the subordinate equity layer of the capital structure. In the event of a disposition, refinancing, or wind-down, LP unitholders receive distributions only after all obligations to Debenture holders have been satisfied in full.

This priority structure enables the LP to raise first-mortgage-secured debt at terms unavailable to unsecured or bank-dependent structures, while providing LP unitholders leveraged participation in portfolio appreciation and distributable income.

Structure — Collective Debentures vs. Bank Act

Debt Structure: Collective First Secured Mortgage Debentures

Traditional joint venture financing relies on institutional mortgage lending regulated under the Bank Act (Canada). Bank-originated mortgages are asset-specific, covenant-constrained, and subject to payout on disposition — proceeds are returned to the lender and must be re-qualified under prevailing bank underwriting standards before redeployment. Capital cannot compound.

Woodfine Direct-Hold Solutions issues First Secured Mortgage Debentures as private debt instruments. These Debentures are:

  • Registered on title as first-charge security against portfolio properties
  • Collective in scope — secured against the LP's portfolio as a whole, not bound to individual assets
  • Outside the Banking Act — not subject to federally regulated lending covenants, approval cycles, or payout requirements on individual dispositions

When the LP disposes of or refinances a portfolio property, proceeds are intended to be retained within the LP structure and redeployed into new acquisitions at the discretion of the General Partner, without bank approval or re-qualification. This recycling mechanism is the structural basis for the LP's growing leverage and expanding square footage in Years 4 through 10 — a compounding dynamic unavailable under conventional joint venture financing.

At identical equity contributions ($250,000,000), the Woodfine Direct-Hold Solutions collective debenture structure is intended to support 41% additional square footage and $244,000,000 more deployed capital than a bank-financed joint venture at equivalent underwriting standards.
