# Product Vision: The PointSavant Co-Location Intelligence Platform

## Mission: To illuminate the commercial power of place.

We are building the definitive platform for understanding and navigating the complex ecosystems of retail and civic co-location. Our product transforms abstract data into tangible, actionable insights, empowering our clients to make strategic, billion-dollar decisions with unparalleled confidence. We are not a map; we are a lens on the future of commerce.

---

## 1. Reimagining the "Bubble": From Dot to Dynamic Beacon

A simple circle is insufficient to represent the rich, multi-dimensional entity of a co-location cluster. We will evolve our core visualization from a static "bubble" to a dynamic "Beacon" that radiates information.

### Visual Language

The design of the Beacon itself will communicate rich, glanceable information:

*   **Size:** Directly proportional to the cluster's proprietary **Power Score**. Larger beacons immediately draw the eye to dominant market nodes.
*   **Color & Fill:** A premium, gradient-based color palette will signify the cluster's Tier (e.g., ★★★★★ to ★). The fill will not be solid but a soft, pulsing glow, subtly conveying the "energy" of the commercial node.
*   **Composition Rings:** The Beacon will consist of 1 to 3 concentric, animated rings. The number of rings will represent the **diversity** of anchors within the cluster (e.g., 1 for retail-only, 3 for a complex mix of retail, civic, and transit). This immediately tells a story about the cluster's character.

### Interaction Model: The "Glance" and the "Inspection"

Our interaction model is designed to reveal information progressively, preventing overwhelm.

*   **On Hover (The "Glance" View):** A minimalist, non-intrusive tooltip appears. It contains only the most critical information:
    *   **Cluster Name:** (e.g., "West Edmonton Power Center")
    *   **Tier Rating:** (e.g., ★★★★★)
    *   **Composition Ribbon:** A clean, horizontal strip of high-fidelity icons representing the key anchor tenants (e.g., Walmart, Costco, Home Depot, Starbucks logos). This provides an instant understanding of the cluster's core components without a single click.

*   **On Click (The "Inspection" View):** Clicking a Beacon triggers an elegant, non-modal side panel that slides into view. This is the deep-dive tool. It is designed for analysis, not just viewing. It will contain:
    *   A detailed list of all primary, secondary, and tertiary tenants.
    *   The core "Cluster DNA" visualization (see Section 3).
    *   Key demographic and trade area data.
    *   A direct, contextual link to the Narrative Wiki ("Map-to-Text").

## 2. The Map <> Wiki Symbiosis: A Seamless Analytical Journey

The Interactive Map is for **exploration and discovery**. The Narrative Wiki is for **explanation and curation**. They must operate as a single, unified product, not two separate destinations.

### "Text-to-Map": Bringing the Narrative to Life

Every mention of a specific cluster or location within a `TOPIC-*` article will be an interactive element.

*   **On Hover:** Mousing over a link like "**Grande Prairie, AB (★★★★)**" in a table will pop up a mini-map preview, showing its geographic location for immediate context.
*   **On Click:** Clicking the link will instantly pivot the user's view. The main map will elegantly pan and zoom to frame the selected cluster, applying a "Spotlight" effect that dims all other Beacons. The narrative context now has a direct, visual anchor, creating a powerful link between the "what" and the "where."

### "Map-to-Text": Weaving Narrative into the Map

When a user is exploring the map, they can instantly access the deep knowledge stored in the wiki.

*   **Contextual Snippets:** The "Inspection View" panel will feature a dedicated "Analysis & Insights" section. This will programmatically pull relevant paragraphs and data points from the wiki articles that mention this specific cluster. For instance, clicking on a Toronto-based cluster might show a quote from the "Top 400 North American Sites" article explaining its unique strengths.
*   **Generated Narrative Summary:** At the top of this section, an AI-powered summary will provide an "on-the-fly" executive brief: e.g., *"This ★★★★★ cluster is a retail-dominant node with exceptional logistics potential due to the co-location of two major warehouse clubs and direct access to the 401 highway."*

## 3. The "Leapfrog 2030" Feature: The Opportunity Engine

Our bold, differentiating feature will be the **Opportunity Engine**, which combines the concepts of "Cluster DNA" and "Lens Mode" to turn our platform from a descriptive tool into a prescriptive, strategic one.

### Part 1: "Cluster DNA" - Visualizing The Present

The centerpiece of the "Inspection View" will be the **Cluster DNA**—a beautiful and intuitive radar chart.

*   **Axes:** Each axis on the chart represents a key category of commercial or civic activity (e.g., "Big-Box Retail," "Warehouse Club," "Medical," "Education," "QSR").
*   **The "DNA" Signature:** The value plotted on each axis is determined by the strength/presence of that category within the cluster. The resulting shape is a unique visual signature—the cluster's DNA. This allows for the immediate, intuitive comparison of two clusters. A user could instantly see that one is a balanced "Community Hub" while another is a spiky, retail-focused "Power Center."

### Part 2: "Opportunity Lenses" - Simulating The Future

This is where we make the leap. Users can activate different **Lenses** to re-analyze the entire map based on a strategic goal.

*   **Activating a Lens:** A user can select a pre-defined lens (e.g., "Logistics & Warehousing," "Family & Community," "Healthcare Hub") or create their own by selecting key DNA components.
*   **Dynamic Re-Scoring:** When a Lens is activated, the map transforms. It is no longer showing our generic Power Score. Instead, it dynamically re-scores every geographic point based on its **potential to satisfy the Lens criteria.**
*   **Highlighting Opportunity:**
    *   Clusters that are a **perfect fit** for the lens will glow brightly.
    *   Crucially, the map will also highlight areas that are **one key anchor away** from becoming a high-tier cluster under that lens. These "Opportunity Zones" will be visualized differently (e.g., a dotted beacon).
    *   Clicking an Opportunity Zone would reveal the insight: **"Add a University campus here to create a potential ★★★★★ Community Hub."**

This feature transforms the platform from a tool that shows *what is* into a powerful simulation engine that shows ***what could be***. It directly answers our clients' most important question: "Where is my next big opportunity?"
