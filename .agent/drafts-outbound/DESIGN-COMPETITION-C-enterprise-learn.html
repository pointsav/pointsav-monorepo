Co-location Tier Methodology

Audience For auditors For engineers
Last reviewed 2026-05-22 View history
Applies to Projects Wiki Documentation Wiki
Status Featured
Share link Cite this article Print / export PDF
Edit article Open discussion

The Woodfine co-location tier methodology classifies retail commercial nodes by the density and diversity of anchor tenant categories, civic service adjacency, and estimated trade area population. The system operates across 13 countries and approximately 5,700 indexed clusters as of May 2026.

1. Overview

The methodology assigns each indexed retail node a tier label (T1 through T4) reflecting its position within a regional market hierarchy. The intent is comparability across jurisdictions: a T2 node in Ottawa should be operationally equivalent — by anchor mix, civic adjacency, and catchment depth — to a T2 node in Bordeaux or Bilbao. Comparability is not market valuation; it is structural classification.

Tier assignment is regenerated quarterly from the underlying source layers and reviewed by the methodology committee before publication. Assignments older than two quarters are flagged in the underlying dataset and re-validated against the most recent administrative boundaries.

Disclosure note. Tier classifications are structural and intended for analytical comparability. They are not investment recommendations, nor do they imply a forward-looking statement about any specific node. Refer to the Regulatory Posture Overview for the full disclosure framework.

2. Tier Definitions

Four tier labels are in active use; a fifth (T0, "metropolitan composite") is reserved and not currently assigned. Anchor density is computed from the ALPHA and GENERIC chain registries, both maintained as separate indexed datasets within the Projects platform.

Tier Label Anchor Density Example
T1 Anchor Composite ≥ 3 ALPHA chains Toronto Yorkdale
T2 Regional Node 1–2 ALPHA chains Ottawa Gloucester
T3 Community Cluster GENERIC chains only Guelph Wellington
T4 Civic-Adjacent Mixed; civic anchor required Kingston Princess

3. Data Sources

The methodology composites three independent data layers: gridded population estimates (WorldPop 2026), origin-destination commuter flows (US LODES 2022; MITMA Spain 2023; equivalent national products where available), and a Woodfine-maintained anchor tenant registry. All three layers carry source citations in the corresponding article entry; license terms are listed in the citation band.

Update cadence

  • Population grid: annual refresh, January.
  • O-D flows: refreshed when the upstream provider publishes; lag of 6–18 months is typical.
  • Anchor registry: continuously maintained; quarterly snapshot is the official version of record.

4. Catchment Methodology

Each indexed node carries a primary catchment polygon derived from the O-D layer. Where O-D data is unavailable for a jurisdiction, a fall-back isochrone is generated from the OpenStreetMap road network (15-minute drive time, off-peak). The fall-back method is flagged on the node record and is excluded from cross-jurisdictional comparability analysis.

5. Limitations

Anchor classification depends on chain self-identification and the registry-maintenance team's monthly review; mis-classified or de-listed chains may persist for up to one quarter. O-D layers do not capture cross-border trips reliably and are excluded for border-adjacent nodes. Population grids carry their own uncertainty bands; tier transitions near a density threshold should not be treated as material movements without analyst review.
