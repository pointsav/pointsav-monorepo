## Recommended lede text

Retail development capital is usually committed to a site before anyone can show the location works. Site selection runs on comparables and local intuition. The convergence of demand at a node — the reason the site should succeed — is assumed rather than measured.

Woodfine Management Corp. operates a retail co-location intelligence platform that measures it. The platform identifies, ranks, and validates commercial real-estate nodes before capital is committed. As of May 2026 it has scored 7,594 co-location clusters drawn from 229,054 analyzed retail locations across 8 retail markets in 13 countries.

Each node is scored on its own spatial evidence, not on pooled market averages. The ranking algorithms weigh the measured convergence of retail demand, and a node is validated independently before it enters the development pipeline. The platform is live at gis.woodfinegroup.com and is built and maintained by PointSav Digital Systems.

This encyclopedia is the methodology reference for that work. It documents the spatial scoring model, the ranking algorithms, the BIM standards, and the regional deployment indices — written for the development firm, the capital allocator, and the programme manager evaluating where retail capital should go.

Forward-looking statements in this wiki reflect current intentions and are subject to risk and uncertainty; they are framed using planned, intended, or target language per the firm's continuous-disclosure practice.
