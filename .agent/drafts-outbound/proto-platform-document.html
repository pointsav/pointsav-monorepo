Economic Model

The economic architecture of the PointSav platform is structured around three tiers: Vendor (PointSav Digital Systems), Customer (enterprise licensees), and Deployment (sovereign instances on customer infrastructure).

Category  Architecture
Status  Ratified
Last reviewed  23 May 2026
Cites  DOCTRINE-CLAIM-12, SYS-ADR-04
Language  EN · ES

For the operational runbook that implements this tiered flow, see Guide: Provisioning a Customer Deployment.

PointSav Digital Systems publishes a single canonical source of truth — the vendor tier — from which all customer-visible artefacts derive. The model is unusual among software platforms in that it makes the chain of custody between vendor and customer a first-class structural concern, rather than a contractual after-thought. Every byte that reaches a customer deployment is traceable to a signed vendor commit; every doctrine claim a customer relies on is anchored to a citation in the vendor's continuous-disclosure ledger.

The vendor does not ship features. The vendor ships a substrate. Features are what customers compose from the substrate on their own infrastructure — and the economic instrument is the right to compose, not the artefact itself.

The three tiers

Each tier has a distinct legal, technical, and economic identity. The boundaries between them are enforced by structure, not by policy.

TierEntityFunctionVisibility
VendorPointSav Digital SystemsSource of truth; publishes substratePublic (GitHub mirror)
CustomerEnterprise licenseeHolds the deployment licence; orders provisioningPer-customer GUIDE catalog
DeploymentSovereign instanceRuns on customer hardware; produces the recordsPrivate to the customer

Flow direction

Information flows in one direction only: vendor → customer → deployment. There is no reverse channel. A customer cannot push code back into the vendor's canonical ledger; a deployment cannot mutate the customer's GUIDE catalog. This unidirectional flow is a load-bearing property of the disclosure model — the vendor's records are stable because no customer can rewrite them.

The mechanism is git topology. Vendor repositories live under the pointsav GitHub organisation. Customer repositories live under woodfine. Deployments live entirely on customer-controlled infrastructure and are never mirrored upstream. The bin/promote.sh tool moves commits from staging through to canonical; nothing moves the other way.

Continuous-disclosure posture

Regulated buyers only. The platform's economic model is calibrated for entities subject to NI 51-102, OSC SN 51-721, and equivalent regimes. Each artefact carries the structural metadata required to be reviewable as a continuous-disclosure record at any moment — without ad-hoc preparation.

For a buyer in financial services, healthcare, or legal practice, the value of the platform is not the application surface but the audit position: every operational record produced by the deployment is, by structure, in a form that withstands regulatory review. The economic instrument is the right to operate inside that posture.

Pricing — three vectors

Customer licences are priced along three independent vectors:

  1. Substrate licence — perpetual right to operate one or more deployments derived from the vendor's canonical tier.
  2. Provisioning — one-time fee per deployment instance, covering the Totebox Session work that delivers the running system.
  3. Continuity — annual subscription that funds vendor-side maintenance: doctrine review, ADR ratifications, and ledger anchoring.

A customer that pays substrate and provisioning, but declines continuity, retains a fully-operating sovereign system. The deployment continues to run; the vendor simply does not push further updates to that customer's GUIDE catalog. This decoupling is deliberate — it ensures that customer operations are never held hostage to vendor-side commercial events.

Air-gap variant

For buyers whose regulators or risk officers require operation without AI, the platform ships a substrate-equivalent air-gap configuration. The deployment runs against local-only services — local-doorman, local-fs, local-fs-anchoring — and never reaches an external endpoint, AI-provider or otherwise. The economic model is identical; the operational shape is reduced.

Settlement

All settlement is on standard commercial terms: invoiced in the customer's jurisdiction, payable to Woodfine Capital Projects Inc. or its designated subsidiary. No tokenised instruments, no consumption-billing, no usage telemetry from the deployment. The vendor charges for the right to operate; the customer operates; the records are the customer's. That is the model.
