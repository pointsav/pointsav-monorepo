# Retail Co-location Methodology

The Woodfine co-location methodology is a deterministic, spatial analysis framework used to identify and rank highly defensible commercial real estate nodes. Analogous to the stringent criteria of the [Michelin Guide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michelin_Guide), the methodology evaluates sites not on subjective potential, but on the proven convergence of independent, capital-intensive retail operators.

## The Named-Anchor Model

The foundation of the methodology is the **Named-Anchor Model**. Large-format retailers deploy sophisticated, capital-intensive site selection criteria before entering a market. When multiple independent operators converge on the same geographic node, that convergence signals a validated, high-quality trade area. 

The system categorizes operators into three distinct tiers:

### 1. Primary Targets
The foundational anchor of any co-location node. A site must contain a Primary Target to be evaluated.
*   **North America:** Walmart Supercentre
*   **Europe:** IKEA (operating assumption)

### 2. Secondary Targets
Complementary large-format operators that drive sustained, multi-purpose foot traffic. They are evaluated within a strict **1.0 km to 3.0 km** catchment radius of the Primary Target.
*   **Secondary-1 (Hardware):** Home Depot, Lowe's, Leroy Merlin
*   **Secondary-2 (Warehouse Club):** Costco, Sam's Club, Makro

### 3. Tertiary Targets
Civic and institutional infrastructure that provides stable, non-cyclical demographic baselines. Evaluated within a **5.0 km** catchment radius.
*   **Tertiary-A (Healthcare):** Hospitals and major medical centres
*   **Tertiary-B (Higher Education):** Universities and colleges

## The 12-Rank Matrix and Quality Tiers

Sites are evaluated using a 12-rank matrix, which maps to five quality tiers. This tiered approach allows for precise differentiation between nodes, identifying those rare locations where all critical commercial elements converge.

### ★★★★★ Tier 5 — Full Co-location
The zenith of commercial validation. The Primary Target is co-located with both a Hardware and a Warehouse Club secondary, and is supported by both Healthcare and Higher Education tertiaries. 

### ★★★★ Tier 4 — Strong Co-location
Complete commercial co-location. The Primary Target is paired with both secondary categories, plus at least one tertiary category. 

### ★★★ Tier 3 — Partial Co-location
The commercial baseline. Includes sites where the Primary Target is paired with both secondaries (but no tertiaries), or one secondary supported by tertiary presence.

### ★★ Tier 2 — Limited Co-location
The Primary Target is co-located with only one major secondary operator. Site quality is highly dependent on local density and tertiary support.

### ★ Tier 1 — Anchor Only
The floor of the index. The Primary Target is present, but secondary co-location is absent.
