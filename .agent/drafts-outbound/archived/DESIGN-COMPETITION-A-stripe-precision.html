Co-location Tier Methodology

The Woodfine co-location tier methodology classifies retail commercial nodes by the density and diversity of anchor tenant categories, civic service adjacency, and estimated trade area population. The system operates across 13 countries and approximately 5,700 indexed clusters as of May 2026.

Last revised 18 May 2026 9 min read 3 citations

Overview

The tier methodology assigns one of three classes — T1, T2, or T3 — to every commercial cluster in the Woodfine index. Assignment is deterministic, derived from a weighted composite of anchor tenant density, civic adjacency (transit, healthcare, education), and the modelled trade area population within a 12-minute drive isochrone.

The framework is intended to support acquisition prioritisation and lifecycle planning. It is not a real estate valuation instrument and does not substitute for site-specific due diligence. Tier assignments are recomputed quarterly against updated anchor tenant and mobility data.

Tier Definitions

T1 Anchor density ≥ 6 categories, civic adjacency present, trade area ≥ 80,000 within 12 min.
T2 Anchor density 3–5 categories, partial civic adjacency, trade area ≥ 30,000.
T3 Anchor density 1–2 categories, no civic adjacency required, trade area ≥ 10,000.

Clusters below T3 thresholds are not indexed. The thresholds are reviewed annually by the methodology committee and are documented in the change log of the tier ruleset repository.

Data Sources

Trade area population is modelled against WorldPop 2026 gridded estimates for international coverage, US LODES 2022 for workplace-area decomposition in the United States, and MITMA ES 2023 for daily mobility origin-destination matrices in Spain. Anchor tenant inventories are sourced from operator filings, licensed retail directories, and direct field validation performed quarterly.

Catchment Methodology

Catchment polygons are computed as 12-minute drive isochrones from the cluster centroid using free-flow traffic assumptions. Where origin-destination data is available, the polygon is reweighted by observed mobility share. Catchment population is then summed from gridded population estimates intersected with the polygon, with a residency-versus-workplace adjustment applied where decomposition data is available.

Limitations

The tier methodology does not account for income distribution, retail spend per capita, or competitor saturation. Coverage is uneven across emerging markets where anchor tenant inventories rely on third-party directories of variable currency. Assignments should be read as a structural index of cluster maturity, not as a forecast of commercial performance.
