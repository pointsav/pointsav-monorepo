Definition: A Regional Market is a named municipality 15–80 km from a major metro centre that contains one or more retail co-location clusters. Markets whose name matches a metro reference (metro-core) and markets more than 80 km from any metro (standalone-secondary) are excluded from the Top 400 lists and scored separately. §4  ·  Scoring Formula

Regional Market Score

score = tier_score × civic_multiplier × confidence
tier_score = (T1 × 4) + (T2 × 2) + (T3 × 1)
civic_multiplier = 1.5 if hospital or university · else 1.0
confidence = 1.0 strong match · 0.7 low-confidence
Formula examples
Plano TX 3T1+2T2+1T3 · civic · (12+4+1)×1.5 25.5
Mississauga ON 2T1+1T2+2T3 · civic · (8+2+2)×1.5 18.0
Chemnitz DE 3T1 · civic · (12)×1.5 18.0
Krefeld DE 2T1 · civic · (8)×1.5 12.0
T1 = Hypermarket + Hardware (or Warehouse Club). T2 = Hypermarket + Hardware, span > 2.5 km. T3 = single-format anchor. Civic = hospital or university cluster member. Score uses the underlying 6,493-cluster database (Phase 23 + Change B, 2026-05-29).

§6  ·  Research Context

The Suburban Ring Gap

Oxford Economics, CBRE, and Colliers International produce extensive research on metropolitan markets — the largest 50–100 cities by GDP and population. The commuter-belt suburbs of those metros, where retail co-location patterns already signal demographic and economic activity, receive substantially less coverage. These 800 ranked markets address that gap.

  • Supply-side signal: Score derived entirely from open-source POI data — no proprietary mobility or consumer-spend panel required. Available across 14 countries simultaneously.
  • Civic anchor validation: All top-5 markets in both NA and EU carry the civic multiplier — hospital and/or university presence — confirming these are community-service nodes, not retail-only zones.
  • Score ceiling differential: NA ceiling (25.5) is 42% higher than EU (18.0), reflecting the larger-format retail park typology in North American suburban markets.
  • Name resolution verified: TIGER 2023 (US), GISCO LAU 2021 (EU), GADM GBR, plus 12 manual CSD overrides for Canada (Sherwood Park AB, etc.).
