Model Notes

  • Cap Rate affects asset valuation and NAV only — it does not change operating cash flows or distributions.
  • Occupancy scales NOI proportionally from Y4. A 10pp occupancy drop lowers both distributions and asset values.
  • Development Yield is NOI as a % of invested capital. Lower yield = lower rents relative to construction cost.
  • Debt Rate scales interest on the debenture schedule. Higher rates reduce coverage ratios and distributable income from Y4.
  • Debt schedule is fixed (Phase 2: $350M gross; Phase 3: ~$987M peak). In practice, higher debt costs might require renegotiation.
  • Benetti Holdings dilution (10%) is held constant. Its economic impact is embedded in the diluted unit count (2,777,777).
  • Income continuity (Y1–Y3) treated as non-cash in this model consistent with the Excel. Cash flow in construction years is driven by equity investment and construction spend only.
  • Outstanding question: the Advisory Fee is modelled flat at $2.5M/year. At 1% of gross funded value (~$1.2B by Y7), the fee could be as high as $12M/year — clarify the fee base with the issuer.
