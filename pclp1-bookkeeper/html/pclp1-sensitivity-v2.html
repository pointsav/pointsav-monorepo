Model Notes

  • Cap Rate affects asset valuation and NAV only — it does not change operating cash flows or distributions.
  • Occupancy scales NOI at the TitleCo building level before rolling up to the LP. Base case is 95%. A 10pp drop from 95% to 85% reduces NOI by ~10.5%.
  • Lease-Up Period reduces NOI in the stabilization year of each phase (Y4, Y6, Y8). A 24-month lease-up reduces stabilization-year NOI by ~50%.
  • Development Yield is NOI ÷ invested capital. TitleCo X as-modelled = 10.02%; TitleCo Y = 10.39%. Both require rent calibration to reach the LP target of 10.5%.
  • Debt Rate scales interest on the fixed debenture schedule (Series A–D, peak $987M). Y7 DSCR of 1.39× is the tightest base-case year; covenant floor is 1.20×.
  • Benetti Holdings dilution (10%) is held constant. Economic impact embedded in diluted unit count (2,777,777).
  • TitleCo X and TitleCo Y are reference specimen buildings from the Grande Prairie proforma. TitleCo X NOI = $1,710,380/yr (63,700 sqft at $41.06/sqft calibrated). TitleCo Y NOI = $2,637,185/yr (114,750 sqft at $35.22/sqft calibrated). Reference entities for disclosure only — not separate LP-level entities.
  • 20-year extension uses approximate values for Y11–Y20 (stabilized portfolio, declining debt ~$7.5M/year). Indicative only; Sprint 2 Rust engine computes exact values.
  • Outstanding question: Advisory Fee modelled flat at $2.5M/year. At 1% of gross funded value (~$1.2B by Y7), fee could reach $12M/year — confirm fee base with issuer.
