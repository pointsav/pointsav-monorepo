Foundry's substrate makes editorial work an audited per-tenant practice.
The four-family taxonomy — PROSE, COMMS, LEGAL, TRANSLATE — composes at
request time with the tenant adapter and the genre template registry.

Three adapters at most per request. Five or more crosses into multi-task
interference territory per the 2025 LoRA literature; the substrate stays
at three by design.

A customer can swap any single service without touching the rest. Replace
the inference router with a customer-owned GPU host while keeping the
data substrate. Replace the data substrate with the customer's existing
knowledge graph while keeping the inference router. The contract between
services is the only thing that needs to hold.

Per-tenant adapters live in the customer's filesystem. Audit ledgers
anchor at the customer's substrate. Continued pretraining runs on the
customer's metal. The trajectory: every editorial action a Foundry-shaped
deployment performs becomes one tuple of training data for the customer's
adapter. The customer's voice deepens over time without their text
ever leaving their substrate.
