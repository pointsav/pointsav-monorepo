PRIVATE CAPITAL PLATFORM OVERVIEW

Bencal Corporation

Province of British Columbia, Canada

Reference Date: 2026-05-29

Bencal Corporation is a British Columbia corporation that organises, capitalises, and administers private capital vehicles for accredited investors. This document is prepared by Management of Bencal Corporation for investor relations purposes and sets out the nature of the firm’s business, its market positioning, its investment vehicle structure, and its corporate platform.

1. Nature of Business and Growth Strategy

1.1 Business Description

Bencal is a Canadian alternative investment manager organised to source, structure, and steward private capital transactions across four strategy families — private credit, real assets, equity, and unconstrained private investments — through a two-entity corporate platform. The platform is composed of Bencal Corporation, the parent holding company, and Bencal Investment Management Inc., the operating manager that holds the firm’s securities registrations, employs its personnel, and acts as promoter and general partner for each underlying vehicle.

Bencal operates exclusively in the exempt market, soliciting subscriptions only from accredited investors and other qualified investors as defined under National Instrument 45-106 Prospectus Exemptions and equivalent foreign regimes. The firm does not offer securities to retail investors and does not participate in publicly-distributed offerings.

The platform is principal-aligned by design. In each vehicle Bencal sponsors, the operating manager holds a co-investment interest alongside its limited partners — typically structured as a 10% unit-base compensation interest — and is subordinated to the return of investor capital on the terms set out in each vehicle’s limited partnership agreement. This alignment is a foundational element of the Bencal economic model: the operating manager is compensated principally when limited partners are compensated, and on terms set deal-by-deal in the relevant offering documents.

The corporate platform sits beneath two personal holding companies, Kiel Capital Inc. and Elzen Holdings Inc., each owned by an individual principal. These personal holding entities are excluded from the operating business and exist for personal tax planning and succession purposes. They are noted here for completeness of the corporate stack and are not part of the investment-management activity described in this document.

1.2 Business Plan

Bencal’s current operating stage is that of a deal-by-deal club deal promoter. Each vehicle is constituted as a separately numbered Canadian limited partnership with its own dedicated general partner corporation, and each subscription is documented under a subscription agreement governed by the accredited-investor exemption under National Instrument 45-106 (and, where applicable, the equivalent foreign-jurisdiction exemption). The current live transaction illustrating this model is the Woodfine Capital Projects Inc. Allocation and Sales Agreement (the “WCP Agreement”), with a base allocation of CAD 3M and a programme cap of CAD 25M, structured on the standard Bencal vehicle economics described in Section 3 below.

The business plan is organised in three planning horizons, each gated to the achievement of an operating milestone rather than to a fixed calendar date. All forward-looking elements below are planned and intended, subject to corporate, tax, regulatory, and commercial considerations, and may be modified, deferred, or not effected.

Near-term targets (planned and intended):

  • Formalise the two-entity corporate structure: incorporate Bencal Corporation as the corporate parent and rename the operating manager to Bencal Investment Management Inc., with the operating manager holding the securities registrations and employee contracts.
  • Build deal cadence across all four strategy families, targeting a first close in each of the Credit, Real Assets, and Equity categories within the first programme year.
  • Formalise an initial exempt-market dealer (“EMD”) referral arrangement to expand the investor base beyond the founding limited-partner network.

Medium-term targets (planned and intended):

  • Achieve an assets-under-management (“AUM”) milestone in the CAD 25M–100M range, at which Bencal intends to formalise structured EMD referral arrangements with one or more national-scale dealers.
  • Progress to an AUM milestone of CAD 100M or greater, intended to support the build-out of an in-house EMD function within Bencal Investment Management Inc. and the addition of Portfolio Manager (“PM”) registration under National Instrument 31-103.
  • Build toward a dedicated distribution team and a structured referral-EMD network across Western Canada, with selective Eastern Canadian and international extension.

Long-term targets (planned and intended):

  • Achieve an AUM milestone of CAD 500M or greater, at which Bencal intends to add Investment Fund Manager (“IFM”) registration under National Instrument 31-103 and may sponsor an evergreen or perpetual-access vehicle designed for the private-wealth channel.
  • Build pan-Canadian distribution and develop qualified United States and European co-investor relationships, supporting cross-border participation in larger Bencal-sponsored vehicles.

1.3 Growth Strategy

The growth strategy is organised around four mutually reinforcing pillars.

First, deal sourcing. Bencal targets proprietary transaction flow originated through the principal network, operating-company sponsor relationships, and co-investor partnerships. The platform is configured to participate as a financial partner alongside operating sponsors rather than as a control investor, and to assemble identified-deal vehicles in which limited partners subscribe with full visibility of the underlying investment before commitment. This sourcing posture is a deliberate alternative to the blind-pool fund model, in which limited partners commit capital to a strategy mandate without knowledge of individual transactions.

Second, distribution network. Distribution is intended to evolve progressively from direct limited-partner relationships in the founding network, through formal referral arrangements with one or more registered EMDs, to an in-house dealer registration held within Bencal Investment Management Inc., and ultimately to a full national EMD and advisor-channel reach. Each step is gated to the AUM and operational milestones set out in Section 1.2.

Third, product expansion. The strategy progression moves from single-deal club deals to programmatic vehicle series in each strategy family, with the eventual addition of an evergreen wrapper for the private-wealth channel. The four-category architecture — Credit, Real Assets, Equity, and Private Investments — is designed to support an investor relationship that is cross-strategy rather than single-strategy, with limited partners able to participate across vehicle categories as their portfolio construction requires.

Fourth, corporate build-out. The naming and structural conventions adopted for Bencal Investment Management Inc. are intended to signal institutional ambition and durability, in order to support talent acquisition and counterparty engagement as the platform scales. Licensing milestones are gated to AUM rather than to elapsed time, and the corporate stack is configured to permit clean separation of regulated and operating functions as the operating manager grows.

2. Market Positioning

2.1 Canadian Alternative Investment Landscape

The Canadian alternative investment market has expanded materially over the five years to 2024. Canadian-domiciled private asset funds held approximately CAD 134B in assets under management in 2023, up from approximately CAD 83B in 2020 — a compound annual growth rate of approximately 17%. The exempt-market dealer ecosystem comprises approximately 300 corporate member firms and approximately 1,500 registered individuals nationally, providing the regulated distribution infrastructure through which most private-asset vehicles are placed.

Family-office allocation to alternatives in Canada has historically lagged global norms. Alternatives currently represent less than 40% of portfolio in approximately 83% of Canadian multi-family-office clients, against a global average of 45–55%. Real estate remains the leading alternative allocation in Canadian family-office portfolios, followed by private equity and — increasingly — private credit, the fastest-growing sleeve in the sector. Private credit has expanded from less than 5% of family-office portfolios five years ago to approximately 10–15% today, and is on a trajectory consistent with continued growth.

The 2025 launch wave of accredited-investor evergreen alternative products by national-scale managers — including the Sagard Private Equity Select Fund, the BlackRock/iCapital alternative platform, the CIBC private-markets product, and the Mackenzie/Northleaf collaboration — signals institutional validation of the accredited-investor channel and the maturation of distribution infrastructure across registered EMDs. The expansion of supply in this segment is broadly aligned with rising demand from a wealthier and more sophisticated Canadian investor base, and supports a near-term outlook in which Bencal is intended to operate.

2.2 Addressable Opportunity

The investor base addressable by a British Columbia-rooted alternative manager is materially underserved by the national-scale Canadian alternative managers, the majority of which are headquartered in Toronto or Montreal. Outside the largest British Columbia-headquartered managers — concentrated in real-estate debt, multi-asset public-market strategies, and growth-stage technology debt — the British Columbia mid-market alternatives field is thin. British Columbia Investment Management Corporation, the public-sector pension manager with approximately CAD 250B in assets, anchors the provincial institutional ecosystem but does not compete for private-investor capital.

Return expectations across the Canadian private-investor base have moderated through 2025, with the blended family-office return expectation converging on approximately 5% on a portfolio basis. The contrast between this moderated expectation and the published target-return ranges in private alternatives — 9–13% yield in private credit and direct lending, 7–10% in real estate debt, 12–18% IRR in value-add real estate equity, 12–16% IRR in value-add infrastructure, and 15–25% IRR in mid-market and growth equity — defines the whitespace into which a credible accredited-investor manager is positioned to operate. Typical minimum investment amounts across these strategies range from approximately CAD 25K at the entry tier of private credit and real estate debt to CAD 250K and above at the institutional tier of mid-market equity and infrastructure, placing the Bencal addressable subscription size within the range of accessible private-wealth and family-office allocation.

2.3 Competitive Positioning

Bencal is positioned along six axes that together constitute the platform’s posture relative to other participants in the Canadian alternative investment market.

Geography. Bencal is British Columbia-rooted and Western Canada-first, with targeted extension into Eastern Canadian and international limited-partner relationships as the platform scales. The geographic posture is designed to address the underserved Western Canadian investor base directly, rather than as a peripheral extension of an Eastern Canadian platform.

Structure. The deal-by-deal club deal model is a deliberate alternative to the blind-pool fund. Limited partners subscribe to identified transactions with full visibility of the underlying investment before commitment, in contrast to blind-pool funds in which limited partners commit capital to a strategy mandate. This architecture is consistent with the Bencal sourcing model — in which identified, often principal-network-originated transactions are the unit of work — and is also consistent with the disclosure preferences of family-office and high-net-worth limited partners who prefer transaction-level rather than fund-level diligence.

Principal alignment. The operating manager holds a co-investment interest in every vehicle Bencal sponsors. Manager economics are subordinated to the return of limited-partner capital on the terms set out in each vehicle’s limited partnership agreement, and the operating manager is principally compensated when its limited partners are compensated. This alignment is structurally enforced through the GP-Inc.-per-deal architecture and the standardised unit-base compensation interest described in Section 3.

Regulatory posture. The platform is institutionally named, connected to the EMD ecosystem from inception, and operates on a progressive licensing roadmap in which the operating manager’s registrations are added as the platform reaches the AUM thresholds at which each registration becomes commercially appropriate. The platform is configured to add each registration category in sequence as AUM milestones are reached.

Product breadth. Bencal operates four strategy families within a single platform: Credit, Real Assets, Equity, and Private Investments. The limited-partner relationship is intended to be cross-strategy rather than single-strategy, with subscribing limited partners able to participate in successive vehicles across categories as their portfolio construction requires. This is in contrast to single-strategy specialist managers, which require investors to maintain separate relationships across strategy specialists in order to access the full alternative-investment allocation.

Access. Minimum investment amounts are calibrated to be accessible across the accredited-investor population, with typical Bencal subscriptions in the CAD 25K to CAD 250K range depending on deal size and category. This positions the Bencal vehicle below the institutional-only thresholds applied at certain national-scale managers and above the small-ticket retail-product offering of public mutual-fund alternatives.

2.4 Competitive Differentiation Summary

Relative to the broader competitive landscape, Bencal is positioned on three differentiating axes:

  • Versus the largest Toronto and Montreal multi-strategy alternative managers operating at scale, Bencal is closer to its limited partners by geography, by transaction visibility, and by principal alignment, and is configured to deliver deal-by-deal participation rather than blind-pool fund commitment.
  • Versus single-deal local syndicators and one-off promoters, Bencal is institutionally constituted, progressively registered, structurally separated between regulated manager and corporate parent, and operates a multi-strategy programme rather than a single-deal practice.
  • Versus the new wave of accredited-investor evergreen products launched in 2025 by national-scale Canadian asset managers, Bencal preserves transaction-level visibility, principal alignment, and direct limited-partner relationship — features that are necessarily attenuated in pooled, perpetually-subscribed, intermediary-distributed evergreen wrappers.

3. Operating Company and Products

3.1 Two-Company Corporate Model

Bencal operates as a clean two-company platform. Bencal Corporation is the corporate parent and Tier 1 entity. It holds the permanent equity interests in the platform and consolidates the operating manager beneath it. Bencal Investment Management Inc. is the operating manager and Tier 2 entity. It holds the securities licence, employs the firm’s personnel, acts as general partner or promoter for each Bencal-sponsored vehicle, and earns the management and carried-interest economics generated by the vehicles.

The separation between parent and operating manager is structurally important. The parent insulates permanent equity capital from the regulated and operating liabilities of the manager. The operating manager — registered under National Instrument 31-103 in the categories appropriate to each AUM stage — bears the regulatory and operating activity of the platform. Personal holding companies Kiel Capital Inc. and Elzen Holdings Inc. sit above Bencal Corporation. These personal holding entities hold the common-share interest in the parent and are excluded from the operating business.

This two-company architecture is consistent with the institutional pattern adopted by national-scale alternative managers, in which a stripped-descriptor parent holds an “Investment Management” or equivalent operating manager. “Investment Management” is the appropriate descriptor for the Bencal operating entity, given the firm’s EMD-and-IFM activity profile, and is distinct from “Investment Counsel,” which carries a wealth-management and segregated-account connotation in the Canadian regulatory register.

3.2 The Four Deal Categories and Naming Convention

Bencal sponsors vehicles in four categories. Each deal receives its own sequentially-numbered general partner corporation and its own sequentially-numbered limited partnership. This per-deal GP-and-LP architecture is the institutional standard for private-fund and club-deal vehicles, and is used universally by national-scale alternative managers including KKR, Blackstone, Apollo, Carlyle, Warburg Pincus, Ares, and Hamilton Lane. Arabic numerals are used consistently across all four categories.

Category 1 — Credit.

GP corporations: Bencal Credit Partners 1 Inc., Bencal Credit Partners 2 Inc., and successive numbered entities per deal.

Limited partnerships: Bencal Credit Partners 1 Limited Partnership, Bencal Credit Partners 2 Limited Partnership, and successive numbered entities per deal.

Scope: private debt, mezzanine, senior secured loans, and structured-income credit transactions. Each Credit deal is constituted as a Canadian limited partnership with its own dedicated GP corporation.

Category 2 — Real Assets.

GP corporations: Bencal Real Assets 1 Inc., Bencal Real Assets 2 Inc., and successive numbered entities per deal.

Limited partnerships: Bencal Real Assets 1 Limited Partnership, Bencal Real Assets 2 Limited Partnership, and successive numbered entities per deal.

Scope: real estate, infrastructure, tangible-asset direct deals, and development transactions. The Real Assets category is the strategy family most closely associated with the firm’s heritage in immovable property.

Category 3 — Equity.

GP corporations: Bencal Equity Partners 1 Inc., Bencal Equity Partners 2 Inc., and successive numbered entities per deal.

Limited partnerships: Bencal Equity Partners 1 Limited Partnership, Bencal Equity Partners 2 Limited Partnership, and successive numbered entities per deal.

Scope: growth equity, co-investment, and direct equity participations in privately-held operating businesses. Bencal participates as a financial partner alongside operating-company sponsors rather than as a control investor.

Category 4 — Private Investments.

GP corporations: Bencal Private Investments 1 Inc., Bencal Private Investments 2 Inc., and successive numbered entities per deal.

Limited partnerships: Bencal Private Investments 1 Limited Partnership, Bencal Private Investments 2 Limited Partnership, and successive numbered entities per deal.

Scope: unconstrained, cross-strategy, and single-investor private transactions that do not fit the first three categories. The Private Investments label is a deliberate choice to signal broad-mandate, unconstrained deal structure — encompassing bespoke single-investor vehicles and direct transactions that do not align to a single strategy family.

The four-category architecture is supported by two stylistic conventions. Arabic numerals (1, 2, 3, and so on) are used consistently across the platform, accurately signalling a club-deal and vehicle series structure rather than a blind-pool fund vintage — the convention generally associated with Roman numeral series in institutional practice. The asymmetric use of “Partners” — present in the Credit and Equity categories and absent in the Real Assets and Private Investments categories — is intentional and consistent with institutional precedent for real-asset and broad-mandate vehicle naming, in which the strategy descriptor is permitted to stand alone.

3.3 Vehicle Economics

Operating manager economics are standardised across all four categories, with deal-specific adjustments documented in each vehicle’s offering and limited partnership agreement.

  • Operating manager interest: 10% unit-base co-investment interest at the limited partnership level, as confirmed by the WCP Agreement and the standard Bencal vehicle structure.
  • Distribution: subscription mechanics are administered through a licensed broker-dealer authorised in the jurisdiction of the direct-hold solution. The current model is an external EMD referral arrangement through Altas One Digital Securities Inc.; the planned model — gated to the AUM milestones described in Section 1.2 — is in-house distribution through Bencal Investment Management Inc.
