## 1. Parent Company

### 1.1 Confirmed name

**`Bencal Private Capital Inc.`**

- Abbreviation: **BPC**
- Role: Corporate parent and permanent capital holder
- Owns: All GP entities, 100% of the operating manager, all limited-partnership interests held at the platform level
- Sits below: `Kiel Capital Inc.` (BCL-CA-01-HLD → **BPC-CA-01-HLD**) and `Elzen Holdings Inc.` (BCL-CA-02-HLD → **BPC-CA-02-HLD**)

### 1.2 Rationale

The research confirmed:

- "Corporation" and "Inc." are the current institutional descriptors at the parent tier. Brookfield Corporation (BN), Ares Management Corporation, Apollo Global Management Inc. all follow this pattern after the 2018–2019 C-corp conversion wave.
- "Private Capital" is the institutional vocabulary replacing "Alternative" and "Investments" at the brand level. It is specific enough to identify the asset class without being stale.
- Brookfield's Dec 2022 split is the reference architecture: parent = "Corporation" (capital allocator); OpCo = "Asset Management" or "Investment Management" (fee earner). Bencal mirrors this: BPC (parent) → Bencal Investment Management Inc. (OpCo).
- "Partners" at the parent level was rejected: it conflicts with "Partners" used in vehicle names (Credit Partners, Equity Partners) and signals a GP entity, not a holding company.
- "Holdings" at the parent level was rejected: "Holdings" is plumbing (internal entities that own GP shares), not a customer-facing brand.

### 1.3 Pre-filing prerequisite

**CIPO trademark search is required before any BC Registrar filing.**

Brighton Park Capital (US/UK private equity) actively uses "BPC" as their abbreviation and owns bpc.com. A CIPO Class 36 (financial services) trademark search for "BPC" and "Bencal Private Capital" is required before committing. If Brighton Park Capital holds a registered Canadian trademark under Class 36, an alternative abbreviation or name variation will be needed.

**BCSC entity search:** Confirm "Bencal Private Capital Inc." is not registered in BC.
**SEDAR+ search:** Confirm no existing issuer with this name or close variant.
**Domain:** bencalprivatecapital.com / bencalpc.com (bpc.com is taken by Brighton Park Capital).

## 3. Vehicle Naming — Four Categories

### 3.1 Category architecture

Research confirmed that category-based series numbering (each strategy has its own counter) is the institutional norm. Unified numbering ("Deal 1, Deal 2, Deal 3" across all strategies) reads as a syndicate operation rather than a multi-strategy manager.

### 3.2 Confirmed vehicle naming schema

Arabic numerals are confirmed. "GP" added to the GP corporation name to explicitly identify entity role in offering documents and regulatory filings.

**Category 1 — Credit**

| Entity type | Name |
|---|---|
| GP corporation | `Bencal Credit Partners 1 GP Inc.` |
| Limited partnership | `Bencal Credit Partners 1 Limited Partnership` |
| Subsequent deals | `Bencal Credit Partners 2 GP Inc.` / `Bencal Credit Partners 2 Limited Partnership` |

Scope: private debt, mezzanine, senior secured loans, structured-income credit transactions.

**Category 2 — Real Assets**

| Entity type | Name |
|---|---|
| GP corporation | `Bencal Real Assets 1 GP Inc.` |
| Limited partnership | `Bencal Real Assets 1 Limited Partnership` |
| Subsequent deals | `Bencal Real Assets 2 GP Inc.` / `Bencal Real Assets 2 Limited Partnership` |

Scope: real estate, infrastructure, tangible-asset direct deals, development transactions.

**Category 3 — Equity**

| Entity type | Name |
|---|---|
| GP corporation | `Bencal Equity Partners 1 GP Inc.` |
| Limited partnership | `Bencal Equity Partners 1 Limited Partnership` |
| Subsequent deals | `Bencal Equity Partners 2 GP Inc.` / `Bencal Equity Partners 2 Limited Partnership` |

Scope: growth equity, co-investment, direct equity in privately-held operating businesses.

**Category 4 — Private Opportunities** *(confirmed 2026-05-31)*

| Entity type | Name |
|---|---|
| GP corporation | `Bencal Private Opportunities 1 GP Inc.` |
| Limited partnership | `Bencal Private Opportunities 1 Limited Partnership` |

Scope: unconstrained, cross-strategy, single-investor bespoke transactions not fitting Categories 1–3.

### 3.3 Category 4 — confirmed

**"Private Opportunities"** confirmed 2026-05-31. Pairs naturally with the parent name "Bencal Private Capital Inc." — the "Private" prefix threads through both the parent entity and the Category 4 vehicles. Consistent with institutional precedent: "Opportunities" is used by Oaktree (Opportunities Fund XII), Ares (Corporate Opportunities VI), Apollo. Signals broad-mandate and cross-strategy scope.

### 3.4 "Partners" asymmetry — confirmed

The asymmetry from the JW2 memo is confirmed and consistent with institutional precedent:
- "Partners" present: Credit (**Credit Partners**) and Equity (**Equity Partners**) — signalling a multi-party GP/LP relationship with active origination
- "Partners" absent: Real Assets and Private Investments — standard for real-asset and broad-mandate vehicle naming

## 4. SPV Naming (Private SPVs — 1 to 3 Investors)

The JW2 memo did not define SPV naming. Research output provides the standard institutional convention.

**Institutional convention for single-deal SPVs:**

| Type | Name pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Named SPV | `Bencal [Codename] Co-Invest LP` | `Bencal Helios Co-Invest LP` |
| Vintage-dated SPV | `Bencal [Category] Co-Invest [YYYY-NN] LP` | `Bencal Credit Co-Invest 2026-01 LP` |
| Matched GP | `Bencal [Codename] Co-Invest GP Inc.` | `Bencal Helios Co-Invest GP Inc.` |

**Codename convention:** Star names, mythological figures, or geographic features are the institutional standard (Sixth Street Lyra Co-Invest LP; KKR Project Aurora Co-Invest LP). The codename is chosen at deal origination and persists as the entity name. It does not reveal the underlying asset or counterparty.

**Key rule:** "Holdings" does not appear in customer-facing SPV or club deal entity names. It is reserved for internal holding entities that hold GP shares but are not visible in offering documents.

The operating manager (`Bencal Investment Management Inc.`) is the entity through which the firm's securities distribution licensing progresses:

| Stage | Milestone | Registration |
|---|---|---|
| Current | WCP co-investment live; referral arrangement with Altas One Digital Securities Inc. | None held in-house |

| Long-term (planned) | AUM CAD 500M+ | IFM registration — NI 31-103; activity-based, triggered by pooled vehicle management |

Unit class convention for accredited-investor vehicles (consistent with Canadian EMD channel standard):
- **Series F** — fee-based class; no trailing commission; standard for accredited-investor and EMD-channel subscriptions
- **Series I** — institutional class; negotiated fee; for subscriptions above CAD 1M
- Series O (optional) — separately managed account class; no distribution component
