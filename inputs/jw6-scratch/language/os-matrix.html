The platform is not one program but nine operating systems arranged in three tiers — so the open-core line is drawn between layers, not bolted on after the fact.

Where licensing falls
✲The base node — ToteboxOS — is FOSS. A single entity runs one archive at no charge; the layers beneath it are open source down to the kernel-to-dependencies rows.
✲The commercial boundary is the Interface OS in the Orchestration tier — stateless multi-archive aggregation is the one proprietary, paid layer. This is the open-core model — per Wikipedia, open-core (GitLab, Grafana).
✲Each layer is its own VM image rather than a process on a shared OS, giving stronger tenant separation than container-on-shared-kernel — per NIST SP 800-190.
✲For investors the matrix is the sovereignty thesis for SMBs: autonomy over data, operations, and infrastructure within a regulatory boundary — a market Gartner sizes at ~$37B sovereign-cloud IaaS growing toward ~$169B by 2028.
