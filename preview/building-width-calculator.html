Section 1The Backwards Method

Most office buildings begin with a width — sixty feet reads well for daylight — then cram furniture in until it fits. Woodfine inverts the sequence. We start with the tenant's actual chair, desk, exam table, lab bench, or lectern, and the building width is whatever that arrangement requires.

The Building Width Calculator is not a width calculator in the conventional sense. It is the output of a backwards-running process that reads furniture inventories, applies the German Circulation Law and the European Lighting Standard, and reports the width that falls out at the end. Use type drives the result: a medical floor plate is wider than an academic one because exam tables are deeper than seminar rows.

The cross-section has three zones, mirrored on each side of a central corridor:

Zone 1 — Habitat

All desks, workstations, exam tables, or benches live here. Six metres minimum from the facade — the European Lighting Standard threshold for natural light at a workstation.

Zone 2 — Magazine

Storage, bookshelves, filing, server rooms, supply closets. Its depth is the slack variable: widen it to keep the building from becoming a long, narrow rectangle.

Zone 3 — Corridor

Bilateral, mirrored at the centre of the floor plate. Width is set by occupancy egress requirements and accessibility — overdesigned by 20% to support small-tenant in-and-out traffic.

The 0.7 m German Circulation Law addition. If desks are perpendicular to the facade, a 6.0 m Habitat depth fits only two desks in series. Adding 0.7 m unlocks the third desk with proper clearance — this is the German Circulation Law in practice. Token: bim.circulation-addition.perpendicular-desk = 0.7 m.

Section 3The Building Width Calculator

The formula is a mirror equation. One half of the floor plate is Zone 1 plus Zone 2 plus Zone 3. The other half is identical. Total building width is the sum.

Zone 1 + Zone 2 + Zone 3 | Zone 3 + Zone 2 + Zone 1 Building Width = 2 × (Habitat + Magazine) + 2 × Corridor   —   or simply 2 × (Z1 + Z2 + Z3)

Where the Corridor is zero (Private Office, Academic — both open to a shared building corridor instead of a bilateral one), the central pair collapses to a single shared corridor and the formula reduces to 2 × (Z1 + Z2).

The ±5 ft precision question. Why does a 1.4 m gap between Medical and Academic widths matter for demising? Because tiles must compose to a single floor plate width across all use types on a floor. If the building is sized for Medical (its widest tenant), Academic tenants get extra Zone 2 they did not ask for. If sized for Academic, Medical does not fit. The tool-buildingwidth engine resolves this by computing the floor's tenant-mix maximum width at design time, then either redistributing Zone 2 depth or constraining tenant mix per floor.

Section 4The Floor Plate

Twenty-thousand square feet of net leasable area per upper floor. Tiles are the assembly units — Small Tile = 2,700 SF, Large Tile = 4,900 SF — and they compose to fill the plate exactly. Three example compositions follow. Hover any tile for its key plan, climate-zone count, and source token.

The rule from the methodology memo: "in all cases, the entirety of the Floor Plates is dividable by a certain combination of Key Plans, with no remainder of free space that is not represented by a Key Plan." Rolling efficiency target: 100%.

Tile = Climate Zone. Each Tile is an independent HVAC zone. A tenant who wants thermostat control leases a Tile. A tenant in a Private Office in Tile B-1 shares climate control with four other private offices — the trade-off the leasing agreement makes explicit. Source token: bim.tile.design-principles.climate-zone-autonomy.

Section 5What Comes Next

This HTML is the specification document for tool-buildingwidth, a Rust engine that will read the DTCG tokens at the right and compute floor plate dimensions, tile compositions, and demising-wall coordinates from a tenant-mix input. The engine outputs IFC-compatible geometry and a leasing-plan summary; this page is what the engine renders as a human preview.

The values shown here are not approximations. Every metre, every square foot, every percentage is read live from the DTCG token files at page load. When the tokens change — when a new key plan sample lands or a corridor width updates — this page changes with them. The engine, when shipped, will share the same source.
